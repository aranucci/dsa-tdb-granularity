2\. Definitions​

Defined terms in this Policy have the following meaning:​

*   Chargeback refers to the situation in which a TikTok Shop customer disputes a TikTok Shop transaction, and the customer requests a return of the sum charged to them under the disputed transaction.​

*   Negative Account Balance refers to when a Seller's account balance has a value below zero. This may occur when the Seller's platform expenses (such as platform commissions) exceed their platform revenues. ​

*   Payment Settlement Period refers to the amount of time between the day an order is delivered to the TikTok Shop customer and the day the funds are released to the Seller for that completed order.​

*   Seller refers to a third-party trader on TikTok Shop.​

*   Unauthorised Payment refers to when a charge is made on a customer's payment method, but the customer did not authorise the charge by any means.​

3\. Payment Settlement Period​

There are 5 types of Payment Settlement Periods available that TikTok Shop may apply:​

*   Introductory Settlement Period: Applies to newly onboarded sellers or sellers in the probation period. Funds are released 31 days from the delivery date.​

*   Standard Settlement Period: The default settlement period that automatically applies to sellers out of probation who do not qualify for faster settlement tiers. Funds are released 8 days from the delivery date.​

*   Accelerated Settlement Period: Offers a faster settlement period than the Standard Settlement Period, with eligibility determined by shop performance. Funds are released 5 days from the delivery date.​

*   Express Settlement Period: The fastest settlement period available on TikTok Shop, assigned based on shop performance. Funds are released 1 day from the delivery date.​

*   Deferred Settlement Period: Applies when TikTok Shop is conducting platform and/or seller security checks, which may extend the settlement period. Funds are released 31 days from the delivery date.​

More information on the eligibility criteria for the different periods can be found [here](https://seller-ie.tiktok.com/university/essay?identity=1&role=1&knowledge_id=375485230499606&from=policy).​

4\. Chargebacks​

4.1. Chargeback Process​

*   TikTok Shop will notify a Seller when a Chargeback request has been raised by a customer in relation to a transaction involving the Seller. TikTok Shop will indicate if the Seller needs to provide information that could help to resolve the Chargeback. The Seller must provide the required information within the period specified in TikTok Shop's notification.​

*   A Seller will be liable for any Chargeback amount, associated fees and compensation for damages that any customer may claim in accordance with applicable law, if the Chargeback is caused by a reason for which the Seller is responsible. ​

*   TikTok Shop will only cover the Chargeback when the customer's payment provider advises that the customer's Chargeback amount was an Unauthorised Payment, in which case TikTok Shop will cover the Chargeback amount and any associated fees. ​

*   If an Unauthorised Payment was caused by suspicious activity on a Seller's part, that Seller is liable for the Chargeback amount and associated fees. ​

4.2. Appeal of Chargeback Claims​

*   To appeal a Chargeback, the Seller must respond in the Seller Center within the timeline specified in the Chargeback notification sent to them. ​

*   The Seller must submit the following information and supporting documents to TikTok Shop to support the appeal: ​

*   The status of the relevant transaction; ​

*   A description of the relevant product(s); and​

*   Proof that the relevant product(s) was/were received by the customer.​

*   Failing to submit the above mentioned documents will result in the Seller's appeal being rejected and that the Seller will be liable for the Chargeback.​

*   The Seller may also submit the following optional information and supporting documents:​

*   The relevant order confirmation email;​

*   Details of any changes to, refunds or cancellation of the order;​

*   Order tracking information;​

*   Images of the product(s) that demonstrate it was sent; or​

*   Customer message records.​

*   All information and documents submitted by the Seller to TikTok Shop in support of an appeal must be accurate and complete. ​

*   Any documents submitted as part of an appeal against a Chargeback may be forwarded to the customer's payment provider and/or any other third party, if necessary.​

*   Once the Seller has submitted their appeal, TikTok Shop will review the documents submitted by the Seller and compile this into a case submission. TikTok Shop will submit the case to the customer's payment provider on the Seller's behalf. Appealing a Chargeback requires TikTok Shop to make the above representation to the payment provider. ​

*   The fee for TikTok Shop carrying out this service is €10 per Chargeback. TikTok Shop has no control over the decision by the customer's payment provider. Accordingly, this service fee will be applied by TikTok Shop regardless of the final decision by the customer's payment provider regarding the appeal. This fee will be deducted from any monies due to the Seller from orders placed on TikTok Shop.​

*   A Chargeback appeal can take up to 3 months to process from the date the Chargeback request is raised by the customer with their payment provider. ​

*   TikTok Shop will inform the Seller of the appeal's outcome in the Seller Center.​

5\. Negative Account Balance ​

*   Sellers must ensure their account does not have a Negative Account Balance.​

*   TikTok Shop periodically reviews Seller accounts and will issue reminders to Sellers with a Negative Account Balance to add funds to their accounts. ​

*   TikTok Shop may take enforcement action against a Seller with a Negative Account Balance.​

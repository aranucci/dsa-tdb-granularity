--------------------

#### What’s the policy?

To ensure a positive user experience and a healthy conversation on the platform, X prohibits the promotion of adult sexual content globally. Advertisements must promote content that is age-appropriate, compliant with market restrictions for certain types of adult content and be mindful about cultural sensitivities.

*   Adult and/or pornographic content. Examples:
    *   Pornographic content in websites, magazines, books, films etc.
    *   Cartoon or computer-generated adult content
*   Full and/or partial nudity depiction. Examples:
    *   Content featuring genitalia, female breasts, buttocks etc
*   Penis enlargement products & services and breast enhancement services
*   Catalogue style dating services. Examples:
    *   Apps / websites with a sign-up process where customers are not required to specify gender or contain profiles that are not customer created or use a pay-per-communication model
*    Sexual entertainment products or services. Examples:
    *   Adult clubs, sexually suggestive online chats, live streamings, games etc.
*   Sexual innuendos including implied sexual content or remarks. Examples:
    *   Sexually suggestive text, images, videos, audio, gestures, poses and movements etc.

**Permitted**

Advertising of below products or services is permitted. Advertisements must observe country specific regulations and local age requirements when promoting such content:

*   Safer sex education and/or STD/STI awareness campaigns
*   Modeled clothing that may include scantily-clad persons (e.g. lingerie)
*   Erotic novels
*   Nude fine art (e.g. Michelangelo's 'David')

*   Medical drawings

Deceptive & Fraudulent Content
------------------------------

This policy applies to monetization on X and X’s paid advertising products.

#### **What's the policy?**

X prohibits the promotion of fraudulent and deceptive content globally. Advertisements are prohibited from promoting the following:

###### Deceptive Content 

*   Ads must not promote products or services using deceptive marketing or misrepresentative business practices. Examples include:
    *   Use of clickbait tactics, where the primary purpose is to drive users to a landing page through exaggerated, sensationalized, inaccurate language or calls-to-action.
    *   Use of misleading tactics or claims during the promotion of a product or service.
    *   Promotion of offers or deals that are not available or omitting vital information on pricing, payment terms, or expenses the customer will incur. 

###### Fraudulent Schemes

*   Ads must not promote content that is associated with fraudulent or scam-like behavior. Examples include:
    *   Tricking users into sending money or sharing personal information without a tangible product or service. 
    *   Offering cash gifts.
    *   Promoting financial scams such as: “get rich quick”, multi-level marketing, pyramid, loan modification, penny stocks, NFTs, Altcoins, or binary option schemes. (see Financial Products and Services for more information)

###### Impractical Outcomes

*   Ads must not contain content that promises or suggests unjustified opportunities or results. Examples include:
    *   Promoting unrealistic claims, results within a specific period, or health or economic opportunities.
    *   Promoting claims or services attempting to change a person’s sexual orientation, gender identity or gender expression.

###### Circumventing Systems

*   Ads must not engage in cloaking tactics, otherwise restricting landing page access, or modifications to URL content following submission to circumvent the review process.

###### Non-existent Features or Functionality

*   Ads must not contain content with non-existent features or functionality. Examples include:
    *   Mimicking X-like features where the functionality does not exist.
    *   Containing excessive redirects on landing pages.
    *   Containing non-functional features or calls-to-action in the ad creative.

*   Accessories associated with drug use
*   Drug dispensaries
*   Depictions of hard drug use

#### How does this policy vary from country to country?

###### Canada

We permit approved cannabis advertisers to target Canada, subject to the following restrictions:

*   Advertisers must be licensed by Health Canada.
*   Advertisers may only promote informational or brand-preference content.
*   Advertisers may only target jurisdictions in which they are licensed to promote these products or services online. For example, advertisers may not promote such content online in Quebec or to Quebec residents.
*   Advertisers are responsible for complying with all laws and regulations. In general, advertisers are prohibited from promoting cannabis content:
    *   by communicating information about cannabis price or distribution;
    *   by doing so in a manner that there are reasonable grounds to believe could be appealing to young persons (anyone under 18);
    *   by means of testimonial or endorsement, however displayed or communicated;
    *   by means of depiction of a person, character or animal, whether real or fictional; or
    *   by presenting cannabis or any of its brand elements in a manner that associates it or the brand element with, or evokes a positive or negative emotion about or image of, a way of life such as one that includes glamour, recreation, excitement, vitality, risk or daring.

Contact X if you are interested in this option.

*   Advertisers must be licensed by the appropriate authorities.

    *   Cannabis cosmetic products licensed by the Thai Food and Drug Administration
    *   Cannabis food and beverages licensed by the Thai Food and Drugs Administration
    *   Cannabis supplements and herbal products licensed by the Thai Food and Drug Administration
    *   Cannabis dispensaries licensed by the Department of Traditional and Alternative Medicine of the Ministry of Public Health.
*   Advertisers may not:
    *   Target customers under the age of 21.
    *   Use creatives which feature the recreational or public use of cannabis.
    *   Advertise products which contain cannabis imported from overseas.
    *   Advertise the sale of cannabis flowers, inflorescence, resin and oleoresin.
    *   Advertise products which contain cannabis extracts exceeding 0.2% THC by weight.

*   Contact X if you are interested in this option.

    *   Exception: Ads for topical (non-ingestible) hemp-derived CBD topical products containing equal to or less than the 0.3% THC government-set threshold.

#### **What's the policy?**

X prohibits the promotion of gambling content, except for campaigns targeting specified countries where it is allowed with restrictions as explained below.

This policy applies, but is not limited, to:

*   Online casinos and gambling, sports betting, and bingo

*   Bonus codes, vouchers, and coupons redeemable for gambling content
*   E-books or websites facilitating or leading to online gambling-related content, including affiliates and other sites driving traffic to gambling
*   Gambling-related software, such as gambling software and poker odds calculators

*   Lotteries

This policy generally does not prohibit:

*   Hotels with a casino, where the focus is on the hotel
*   Gambling accessories, such as poker chips or pachinko machines

Unacceptable content
--------------------

#### What is the policy?

X prohibits ads that contain unacceptable content globally. While all content on X is subject to the [X Rules](https://help.twitter.com/en/rules-and-policies/twitter-rules), we place additional restrictions on advertising content.

Categories of content we consider unacceptable for advertising include:

###### Derogatory Content

Ads must not promote content that is disrespectful, discriminatory, or harmful towards an individual, group, or organization, including hate speech or advocacy against protected groups, individuals, or organizations based on characteristics such as: race, ethnicity, color, national origin, sexual orientation, sex, gender identity, religious affiliation, age, disability, medical or genetic condition, status as a veteran, status as a refugee, status as an immigrant. Examples: 

*   Inflammatory or provocative content that incites anger or violence
*   Content that seeks to exploit to gain unfair advantage
*   Offensive, abusive, demeaning, intimidating or bullying content against an individual or a group
*   Promotion of, support for, or affiliation with, organizations, groups or individuals associated with promoting hate, criminal and/or terrorist-related content
*   Degrading, mocking, or harassing references to events or practices that negatively affected a protected group

###### **Inflammatory, Profane, or Offensive Content**

Ads must not promote content that is inflammatory, profane or offensive for the general audience. Examples:

*   Content that promotes violence
*   Vulgar and profane content including words obscured by asterisks, symbols, etc.
*   Shocking or distasteful content
*   Dangerous content or content likely to cause injury or harm

###### Harmful Weight Loss or Body Image Content

Ads must not promote weight loss content that impacts physical and mental health and body image. Examples:

*   Content that is reasonably considered to 'body-shame' the customer.
*   Content that encourages, glamorizes, or promotes unhealthy or unsafe eating behaviors or eating disorders

###### Sensitive Event & Controversial Issues

Ads must not profit or exploit a sensitive event, topics, or controversial issues such as natural/industrial disasters, violent attacks etc. that have significant social or political impact. 

###### Unauthorized References

Ads must not imply or suggest unauthorized connections to brands, products, services, or platforms without authorization. Examples:

*   Claims of affiliation, endorsement or integration with our products or services without permission. 
    
*   References to other products or services in a way that positions them as alternatives to, or closely associated with, our offerings where no such relationship has been approved.
    
*   Unauthorized use of our proprietary terminology, product, feature names, branding, logos, or similar elements that could create confusion about an official partnership or endorsement.
    
*   Implied platform access, special privileges, or claims involving our internal tools, data, algorithms, moderation processes, or review systems.

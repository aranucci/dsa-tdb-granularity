AliExpress reaffirms "seller's obligation to declare truthfully"

Dear Sellers,

According to relevant laws and regulations, sellers should voluntarily fulfill (or urge consumers to fulfill) tax and customs clearance obligations. Sellers must not understate, conceal, or falsely report transaction information at the import and export stages, nor help buyers evade or pay fewer taxes. Such violations include but are not limited to making statements or implications in store pages, product descriptions, or other channels of communication with buyers that various illegal tax avoidance operations can be carried out.

**Examples of violation information include but are not limited to:**

**·** avoid tax

**·** declare lower value

**·** avoid value-added tax

**·** tax evasion

**·** declared the item as a gift

To demonstrate the platform's prohibition attitude towards suspected illegal behavior, the Global AliExpress platform rules will add an item (8) to the "Illegal Profit" rule: For sales purposes, sellers are suspected of engaging in or assisting buyers in various illegal tax avoidance behaviors in any explicit or implicit manner. This modification will take effect from January 20, 2020.

Sellers are requested to thoroughly check and inspect their stores' product information. If there is any violation information mentioned above, please immediately delete and rectify it. The self-rectification period for sellers starts from the date of this announcement until January 20, 2020. After the self-rectification period, the platform will further strengthen the investigation of such violation information. Once discovered, the relevant products will be deleted, and 0.5 points will be deducted. In severe cases, the platform reserves the right to freeze or close the account.

Thank you for your attention and cooperation.

AliExpress

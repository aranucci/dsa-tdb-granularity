AliExpress Explanation of the Assessment Criteria for "Transaction but Not Selling"

Effective Date: December 2, 2020, Beijing Time

Dear sellers,

It is the basic obligation of sellers to strictly adhere to the promised price, delivery time, logistics services, and product quality. In order to maintain a healthy and orderly marketplace, enhance the shopping experience of buyers on the platform, and help sellers better provide services to consumers, the following changes are made to the assessment criteria for "Transaction but Not Selling":

"Transaction but Not Selling" refers to the behavior of sellers failing to ship the order after the buyer has made payment. The aforementioned delay refers to the failure to upload the tracking number before the seller's shipping deadline (including cases where partial shipment is made but not all items are confirmed as shipped before the seller's shipping timeout), resulting in the order being closed by the system. Note: If the cancellation of the order is initiated by the buyer, it will not be counted as "Transaction but Not Selling" in the assessment. Please refer to the "Global AliExpress Transaction but Not Selling Rules" for more details.

**FAQ:**

Q: What is the current cancellation process in general?

A: If a consumer requests to cancel an order, the seller should respond within 72 hours. If there is no response from the seller within 72 hours, the order will be closed due to timeout, and the payment will be refunded to the buyer. The order status will be displayed as "Order Closed." Note: Buyer-initiated cancellations before payment do not require seller confirmation and will be automatically closed by the system.

  

Q: Will agreeing to a buyer's cancellation request have any impact on my store?

A: If the cancellation of the order is initiated by the buyer, any reasons for canceling the order will not have any impact on your store (it will not be counted as "Transaction but Not Selling"). Note: You do not need to guide the consumer to modify the cancellation reason. On the contrary, you can identify the shortcomings in store management through the reasons for order cancellations provided by consumers, which can help you better operate your store.

Q: Why was the order automatically closed even though I did not agree to the buyer's cancellation request?

A:

（1）If you do not respond within 72 hours, the order is affected by the cancellation rules and will be automatically closed.

（2）If you have engaged in some violations, the platform may close the order. For example, if the consumer complains about your lack of stock, price increase, refusal to ship, etc., and the platform verifies the evidence and finds the situation to be true, the order will be closed.

（3）For unpaid orders, buyer cancellations do not require your confirmation and will be automatically closed by the system.

Q: Does this apply to all orders or only to new orders?

A: This applies to all orders.

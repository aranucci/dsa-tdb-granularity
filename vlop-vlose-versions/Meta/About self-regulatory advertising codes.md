About self-regulatory advertising codes
=======================================

It’s an advertiser's responsibility to understand and comply with the policies outlined in Meta's [Advertising Standards](https://transparency.fb.com/policies/ad-standards/), our Terms of Service and any other applicable terms and guidelines. In addition, all advertisers are expected to comply with the local laws and regulations for any country that their ads target.

Note that certain countries, industries and self-regulatory organizations (SROs) have their own self-regulatory codes for advertising and marketing.

For example, the [ICC Advertising and Marketing Communications Code](https://iccwbo.org/publication/icc-advertising-and-marketing-communications-code/#:~:text=The%20ICC%20Marketing%20Code%20seeks,and%20truthful%20communications%20and%20practices.), published by the International Chamber of Commerce, sets out standards for a range of marketing activities, including digital marketing communications, and is used as a baseline set of rules for responsible marketing in many countries.

A non-exhaustive list of self-regulatory advertising codes is included below for reference. These codes have been developed independently of Meta. In addition to complying with Meta’s policies, advertisers are expected to do their own research on codes that may apply in the country they are seeking to advertise in.

Beneficiary and payer requirements for ads targeting the European Union
=======================================================================

Due to regulatory requirements in the European Union, as of June 5, 2023, if your Facebook or Instagram ads target the EU or its associated territories listed below, or if you select worldwide as the location, you have the option to enter the person or organization who is benefiting from the ad (“beneficiary”). You also have the option to enter the person or organization paying for the ad (“payer”), if that person or organization is different from the beneficiary.

Beneficiary and payer information is required for all new or edited ads targeting the EU or its associated territories. Without this information, your ad cannot be published.

This requirement applies to all ad creation tools across Facebook and Instagram, such as Ads Manager, Meta Business Suite, Ad Center, Instagram ads and the Marketing API.

This information will be available in the [Meta Ad Library](https://www.facebook.com/ads/library/?active_status=all&ad_type=political_and_issue_ads&country=US&media_type=all) but will not be displayed on the ad itself or in feed. It will remain in the Meta Ad Library for one year after it serves its last impression. While you are required to add this info to all ads targeting the European Union or associated territories, adding this information will not have any impact on how the ads are delivering, cost or bidding, feature accessibility or the ad review process.

**Note**: All beneficiary and payer information will be recorded in the Ad Library. If you edit your beneficiary or payer information, Meta will archive all versions for one year.

Who to enter as the beneficiary and payer for my ad
---------------------------------------------------

In all cases, it is up to the advertiser to comply with any applicable laws and regulations in the country they want to run ads in.

The EU Digital Services Act (DSA) requires Meta to provide information on “the natural or legal person on whose behalf the advertisement is presented” (the beneficiary of the ad) and, where different, “the natural or legal person who paid for the ad” (the payer of the ad).

Some examples could include:

*   If you are a small business and running ads on your own, your beneficiary could be the name of your organization and your payer could be the same.
    
*   If you are a subsidiary of a parent company, your beneficiary could be your subsidiary name or brand name, and the payer could be the parent company.
    
*   If you are an individual promoting a specific product, service, candidate, etc., your beneficiary could be the name of the product, service, candidate, etc., and the payer could be your name.
    

How to enter a beneficiary and payer
------------------------------------

When your ad targeting includes the EU or associated territories, you are required to provide a beneficiary and payer for the ad:

1.  You can add or change your default beneficiary and payer at the ad account level by going to [Advertising settings](https://adsmanager.facebook.com/adsmanager/manage/advertising_settings/digital_services_act) in Ads Manager. This information will automatically be used when creating new ad sets in that ad account. In order to manage the default beneficiary and payer information in Advertising settings, you need to have full control of the ad account.
2.  You might see that beneficiary and payer information has already been added when you create a new ad set or ad or edit an ad set or ad. We selected your Facebook Page or Instagram profile name as the beneficiary and payer. Review the autofilled information and make any edits when needed in **Advertising settings**.
3.  When choosing a beneficiary, advertisers could see several options:
    *   If applicable, advertisers can choose from up to 10 “Recently used” designations that have been used when creating ads in that ad account.
        
    *   If applicable, advertisers can choose from options that were previously verified by Meta and are associated with that advertiser, including:
        
        *   [The name of a business that was verified in Meta Business Suite](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/2058515294227817)
            
        *   The organization name in a [verified disclaimer](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/488070228549681) to run ads about social issues, elections or politics
            
        *   [A confirmed business or organization that has claimed ownership of a Page](https://www.facebook.com/help/323314944866264)
            
        *   An organization that has been verified with a [Financial Conduct Authority firm reference number](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/719892839342050)
            
        *   The business name of a [seller who was verified](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/330763671765280) due to certain regulatory requirements
            
        
    *   Advertisers can manually type in another entity. Beneficiary and payer information must comply with Meta's Community Standards and Advertising Standards, if applicable.
        
4.  After entering a beneficiary, you can switch on a toggle that reads “The beneficiary and payer are different.” If you turn on this option, you'll see similar options as when choosing a beneficiary and you'll need to select one or manually type in another entry for the payer.  
      
    If the beneficiary and payer are the same, you don't need to turn on this option.

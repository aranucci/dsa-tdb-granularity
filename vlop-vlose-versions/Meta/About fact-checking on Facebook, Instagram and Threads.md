About fact-checking on Facebook, Instagram, and Threads
=======================================================

We're committed to addressing misinformation on Facebook, Instagram and Threads. In many countries and regions, we work with independent third-party fact-checking organizations who are certified through the non-partisan [International Fact-Checking Network (IFCN)](https://ifcncodeofprinciples.poynter.org/) or [European Fact-Checking Standards Network (EFCSN)](https://efcsn.com/code-of-standards/) to identify, review and take action on this content. In the United States we have a community-based program called Community Notes. We are beginning with rolling out Community Notes in the US, and will continue to improve it over the course of the year before expansion to other countries. Learn more [here](https://transparency.meta.com/features/community-notes).

**Note:** Our [misinformation policies](https://transparency.meta.com/policies/community-standards/misinformation/) remain in place, such as removing misinformation when it is likely to directly contribute to the risk of imminent harm.

Read more [about our fact-checking partnerships](https://www.facebook.com/formedia/mjp/programs/third-party-fact-checking) and learn about the elements of how our program works outside the United States in the following articles:

*   [Rating options for fact-checkers](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/341102040382165)
    
*   [Program policies](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/315131736305613)
    
*   [Enforcement of fact-checker ratings](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/297022994952764)
    
*   [Request a review of a fact-check rating on Facebook, Instagram, and Threads](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/997484867366026)
    

The focus of this fact-checking program is to identify and address viral misinformation, particularly clear hoaxes that have no basis in fact. Fact-checking partners prioritize provably false claims that are timely, trending and consequential [in the countries and languages they cover](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/997484867366026).

Fact-checking partners should not rate content with claims that are inconsequential, contain minor inaccuracies, or media that is only altered for quality or clarity. Additionally, the program isn't meant to interfere with individual expression, opinions and debate, clearly satirical or humorous content, or business disputes.

How the program works outside of the United States
--------------------------------------------------

Our program includes several key steps:

*   **Identify false news**: We identify potential misinformation using signals, like feedback from people on Facebook, Instagram, and Threads, and surface the content to fact-checkers. Fact-checkers may also identify content to review on their own.
    
*   **Review content**: Fact-checkers will review content, check facts, and rate the accuracy of the content. This happens independently from Facebook, and may include calling sources, consulting public data, authenticating videos and images and more.
    
*   **Clearly label misinformation and inform people about it**: Meta labels content that’s been reviewed by fact-checking partners, so people can read additional context. We also notify people before they try to share this content and people who have shared it in the past.
    
*   **Make sure fewer people see misinformation**: After a fact-checker rates a piece of content as **False**, **Altered** or **Partly False**, it will receive reduced distribution on Facebook, Instagram, and Threads. For this content, as well as content fact-checkers rate Missing Context, it will be filtered from recommendations and content that Meta suggests. This significantly reduces the number of people who see it. We also reject ads with content that has been rated by fact-checkers.
    
*   **Take action against repeat offenders**: Pages, Groups, Profiles, websites and Instagram accounts that repeatedly share misinformation will face some restrictions, including having their distribution reduced. This includes content rated **False** or **Altered** by fact-checking partners; content that is near-identical to what fact-checkers have debunked as false or altered; and content we remove against under our [Misinformation Community Standard policies](https://transparency.meta.com/en-gb/policies/community-standards/misinformation/). Repeat offenders may also have their ability to monetize and advertise removed and their ability to register as a news Page removed for a given time period.
    

What fact-checkers are not responsible for
------------------------------------------

Meta may take a number of different actions on content or accounts that go against our policies - both policies about actions we take when a fact-checker rates content as false and separate policies like those about what is and isn’t allowed on our platforms. Fact-checkers are not responsible for these actions which include but are not limited to:

*   Processing review requests on [Community Standards](https://transparency.fb.com/policies/community-standards/) violations. You can request a review of Community Standards violations on Facebook [here](https://www.facebook.com/help/2090856331203011) and Instagram or Threads [here](https://www.facebook.com/help/instagram/280908123309761).
    
*   Removing content from Facebook, Instagram, and Threads.
    
*   Removing or blocking Pages and accounts.
    
*   Reducing the distribution of false information on Facebook and Instagram.

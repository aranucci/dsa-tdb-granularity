X Ads Policy Update Log

The X Ads policies evolve to reflect what’s happening in the world and on the X platform. This Ads Policy Update Log compiles these ongoing changes to our policies.

Typically, this log will reflect policy changes impacting what is and is not permitted to be advertised via X Ads. For example, policy updates prohibiting a certain business model or the addition of country-specific policies may be included. Structural changes, such as grammatical or formatting edits, may not.

This log does not replace our policies, and advertisers should always review the pertinent policy pages for any restrictions or additional information about our policies. Additionally, translations are provided as a convenience, and are not meant to change the content of our policies. The English version will control and should be your reference in case of conflict between a translation and the English version.

For complete information about our Ads Policies, visit [x.com/adspolicy](

In response to the unprecedented COVID-19 pandemic, X Ads Policies are evolving to meet changing global circumstances.

[Safe for Ads Content Guidelines]( Defined the COVID-19 pandemic as a sensitive event. As a result, content that discusses or focuses on COVID-19 is not eligible for monetization through the Amplify Publisher Program.

[Inappropriate Content]( Updated the existing Inappropriate Content policy, as it pertains to COVID-19 specifically:

\- Advertising of facemasks and alcohol hand sanitizers is prohibited globally.

\- COVID-19-related Public Service Announcements (PSA’s) from government and supranational entities are permitted.

\- News outlets that currently hold a political content [exemption]( are permitted to advertise content around COVID-19.

\- Some vetted organizations who have a current partnership with the X Policy team are permitted to advertise content around COVID-19.

[Inappropriate Content]( Misleading synthetic or manipulated content, and the appropriation of sensitive event-related hashtags are prohibited.

[State Media]( Advertising of content from news media entities controlled by state authorities is prohibited.

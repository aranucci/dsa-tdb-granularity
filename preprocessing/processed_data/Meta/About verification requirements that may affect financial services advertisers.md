About verification requirements that may impact financial services advertisers

![](

Beginning July 28, 2025, all advertisers running securities and investments ads in India, will need to comply with Meta’s verification and transparency requirements.

To help prevent fraud and impersonation in financial products or services advertising, and in some cases to comply with regulatory authorities, Meta may ask you to verify information about yourself or your organization in order to publish ads that promote financial products or services. These requirements are intended to promote consumer safety.

Financial services ads generally promote financial products and services, subject to unique regulatory requirements in each country. Financial products and services that may require a license include but are not limited to:

Insurance products

Mortgages

Loans (long and short-term)

Investment products and opportunities

Credit card applications

Generally, advertisers can run the following types of ads without verification, provided that the ad does not focus on an in-scope financial product or service:

Brand ads for banks and insurance companies

News articles related to financial products or services

Information about financial education

Education, training or skill building to apply for loans or manage them

Educational advertisements about a Financial service or product that does not provide the ability to obtain or connect with that product or service

Advertisers attempting to run ads promoting financial products and services targeting audiences in the following regions require verification:

Australia

India

Taiwan

United Kingdom

Refer to the sections below to learn more about specific verification requirements and processes.

**Requirements for each region may be significantly different.**
Australia

![](
Requirements to run financial services ads in Australia

Meta requires advertisers to complete the following verification and on-ad disclaimer steps to publish ads covered by the [Australia Online Scams Code](

**Verify the ad’s beneficiary and payer:** Before running eligible financial services ads, advertisers will need to verify information about the ad beneficiary and payer, including their Australia Financial Services License information (unless otherwise exempt). Generally, the beneficiary is the individual or organization benefiting from the ad, whereas the payer is the individual or organization paying for the ad.

**Select verified information to disclose:** Eligible financial services ads will need to contain a **Paid for By** disclaimer, featuring the name of the individual or organization paying for and/or benefitting from the ad. The clickable disclaimer leads to a separate information sheet with verified information about the beneficiary and payer, as well as the license number or exemption. This disclaimer will be created during the ad creation process, according to your nominated beneficiary and payer information.

**Note:** This verification information will be displayed publicly on the ads when they are running and for up to 7 years in Meta’s Ad Library.

Frequently asked questions
I am not sure if I am the “beneficiary” or the “payer.” Can you please advise me?

The ‘beneficiary’ of an ad is the individual or organization benefiting from the ad, while the ‘payer’ is the individual or organization paying for the ad. As Meta is not in a position to provide advice on the specific commercial arrangements between individuals or organizations in respect of ads, Meta encourages advertisers to work with their legal representatives to advise on the appropriate designations to use.
I want to target a global audience. Does this mean I need to follow the requirements of the Australia Online Scams Code?

Yes, to deliver ads for financial products or services to a global audience, you will need to follow the verification and disclosure requirements under the Australia Online Scams Code. Learn more about [authorization requirements](
My business is not located in Australia. Do I still need to follow the verification and disclaimer requirements for ads that target users in Australia?

Yes, to publish qualifying financial products or services ads targeting users in Australia, you’ll need to adhere to our Australia-based verification and disclaimer requirements.

**Note:** The verification process provides an option to self-declare as exempt from requiring an Australian Financial Services License (AFSL). Advertisers that qualify for an exemption within the specifications of the Australia Online Scams Code will be able to self-select as exempt. As the Australian financial services licensing regime is a regulatory framework, Meta is not in a position to provide advice on compliance with the regime. We recommend that you consult with the Australian Securities and Investment Commission and/or your legal advisors to confirm whether you are exempt from requiring a licence. Advertisers that self-select as exempt from requiring an AFSL will have an additional disclaimer featured on their ads, **AFS license: exemption claimed**.

Select the applicable beneficiary information from the dropdown list. If the payer information is different from the beneficiary, select a distinct payer from the dropdown.

**Note:** You cannot edit a disclaimer once the ad is live. The ad will need to be taken down.
Important information

If you provide false or misleading information during the verification process, attempt to claim and verify a business you’re not authorized to represent, or otherwise attempt to circumvent Meta’s verification review system, Meta may prevent your business from being verified, remove your business's verified status, or take additional action on the account.

Frequently asked questions
I have already completed business verification. Does this verification still apply to me?

If you have already verified your business, you may be eligible to reuse verified information to select as the beneficiary or payer of your ads. As a next step, you should (1) check to see that this information is visible to you (select [Authorizations and verifications]( tab in Meta Business Suite and then click **Verification for ads delivered to Taiwan** to see what information has already been verified) and (2) confirm that this information accurately reflects the beneficiary and payer of your ads.
I think my ad qualifies as financial services, but it was rejected. What should I do in this case?

If your ad was incorrectly rejected, you can request another review in **Business Support Home** by clicking **Request review**. Clicking this button will start another review of the ad against our policies to ensure that it was correctly rejected.
Who is the ad “beneficiary”? How is it different from the ad “payer’?

The ad beneficiary is the person or organization that benefits from the ad running, generally the company that owns the product or service the ad is promoting, though in some cases could be another third party (example: a charity). The ad payer is the person or organization that sponsors the ad.
Who is eligible to verify?

Only Page Admins are eligible to verify information about the people or organizations benefiting from and paying for ads.
I am an advertiser and my ad needs a disclaimer, but I am not the beneficiary or the payer. What should I do?

First, check to see whether the beneficiary and/or payer information has already been verified. To check whether there is existing verified information that you can use for your disclaimers, visit the [Authorizations and verifications]( tab in Meta Business Suite, then click **Verification for ads delivered to Taiwan**. If there’s already verified information about the people or organizations associated with your ads, you will see their names listed.

If not, you will need to verify the information before running your ad. If the beneficiary and/or payer is an organization that you are tied to, you may verify the organization’s information. If the beneficiary and/or payer is another individual, you will not be allowed to upload an ID on their behalf. Alternatively, you may advise the beneficiary to complete this step. **Note:** They’ll need to be an account admin in order to verify.
I want to target a global audience. Does this mean I need to follow the requirements of the Taiwan law?

How to get verified to run financial services ads in the United Kingdom
Confirm your authorization status

Any advertiser that wants to run ads promoting financial products or services that target UK audiences needs to be authorized with the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA). To learn more, [visit the FCA website](
Before you begin

If you try to publish an ad that promotes a financial product or service to audiences in the UK and see a message that says your ad was rejected because it doesn’t comply with Meta’s policy for financial services advertisers, you may need to confirm that you are authorized by the FCA before you can publish your ad.

To confirm you are authorized, you’ll need:

A Meta ad account.

Your FCA firm reference number (FRN): If you haven’t gone through the authorization process with the FCA, you’ll need to complete it to receive your reference number.

An email address with a domain that matches the website address listed in the FCA authorization **or** the phone number associated with the FCA authorization. An example of an email address with a matching domain is "user@meta.com" and "www.meta.com."

**Note:** If you’re an advertiser on Instagram and don’t have an ad account, you’ll need to [link one to your Instagram account.](
Provide your information

In Meta Ads Manager, open the notification that asks you to confirm your authorization with the FCA.

Alternatively, go to [Meta Business Support Home]( click **See violations** for the rejected ad and click **Verify now**.

Click **Get started** and enter your FCA firm reference number.

If your organization is found in the FCA’s [Financial Services Register]( we’ll ask you to confirm the phone number or email address that is associated with the record.

Select a method to receive the confirmation code: email, phone call or text message (SMS).

Once you receive the confirmation code, enter it and submit it.

When you’re done, you’ll receive confirmation through an on-screen message and email.

**Note**: If you believe your ad was incorrectly rejected because it isn’t about financial products or services or it doesn’t target audiences in the UK, you can request another review in Business Support Home. You can click **Request review** to start another review of the ad against our policies.
